So you want to know how Arizona actually works.

Ever wonder how 90 people in a building in downtown Phoenix decide the rules for 7.4 million Arizonans? Welcome to the sausage factory.

This site breaks down how the Arizona Legislature works in plain English. No jargon-filled textbooks, no poli-sci lectures, no pretending it's simple when it's not. Just the real process... the messy, fascinating, occasionally infuriating way laws get made in this state.

Whether you're here because you saw a bill on the news, you want to testify on something that matters to you, or you just want to be a more informed voter, you're in the right place.

Things Most Arizonans Don't Know

The Arizona Legislature meets for roughly 100 days a year. The Arizona Constitution, Article IV, Part 2, Section 1 says the regular session starts on the second Monday in January.

Arizona has 90 lawmakers: 30 Senators and 60 Representatives. Each of the 30 legislative districts elects 1 senator and 2 representatives. See the current roster at azleg.gov.

The governor gets 5 days to sign or veto a bill (10 days if the legislature has adjourned). If they do nothing, the bill becomes law automatically; that's called a "pocket signature." Arizona Constitution, Article V, Section 7.

In 2025, Governor Hobbs set a modern record by vetoing over 170 bills in a single session. Divided government makes for a busy veto pen.

Any Arizonan can testify on any bill through the Request to Speak (RTS) system. For free, from your couch. If you've never used it, it's genuinely one of the most powerful civic tools in the state.
